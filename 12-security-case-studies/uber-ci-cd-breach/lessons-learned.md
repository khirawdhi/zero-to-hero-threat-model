# Lessons Learned

The Uber incident highlights the importance of strong identity security and internal system protections.

## Zero-Trust Architecture

Organizations should adopt zero-trust principles where internal access is continuously verified rather than assumed to be trusted.

## Identity Protection

Strong identity controls can reduce the risk of credential compromise.

Examples include:

- multi-factor authentication
- phishing-resistant authentication
- device verification

## Least Privilege Access

Users and systems should only have access to resources necessary for their roles.

Limiting permissions reduces the impact of credential compromise.

## Monitoring and Detection

Organizations should monitor internal systems for unusual access patterns and privilege escalation activity.

Early detection can prevent attackers from moving laterally across infrastructure.

## Security Awareness

Social engineering remains one of the most common attack vectors.

Regular security training can help employees recognize and report suspicious activity.
